Charle's Law

Charles’ law - a balloon is inflated to a volume of 2.0L at a temperature of 25°C.
If the balloon is taken outside on a hot summer day, what will be the new volume of the
balloon if the temperature increases to 50°C?





V1 = 2.0L
V2 = 2.17L
T1 = 25C – 298K
T2 = 50C – 323k
=(2.0L) (323K) 298K
Volume = 2.17L is the answer

Gay-Lussac Law

Gay-Lussac’s law - a LPG tank used for cooking has a pressure of 2.0 atm at a temperature of 298 K.
What will be the new pressure if the tank is left outside in the sun, and the temperature increases to 323 K?





P1 = 2.0 atm
P2 = 2.17 atm
T1 = 298 K
T2 = 323 K
= (2.0 atm) (323K) 298K

Combined Law

A sample of nitrogen gas initially occupies a volume of 10 liters at a temperature of 25°C and a pressure of
1 atm. If the volume is decreased to 5 liters and the temperature is increased to 50°C, what will be the new
pressure of the gas?





P1=1 atm
V1=10L
T1=298K
P2=?
V2=5L
T2=323K
P2=P1V1T2÷V2T1
=(1atm)(10L)(323K)÷(5L)(298K)
P2=2.17K












Avogado's Law

if a container carries 2.0 moles of gas with a volume of 55L, what will be the new volume
of the gas in the container if the number of moles is increased to 4?





V1 = 55L
V2 = 110L
N1 = 2.0 moles
N2 = 4 moles
= (55L) (4 moles) / 2.0 moles
= 110 L
